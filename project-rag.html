Executive Overview

A Business-First AI System for Recall Readiness

The Ford Recall Readiness Co-Pilot is designed around a serious enterprise problem: vehicle failure signals often emerge across many disconnected systems long before they become formal recall decisions. Warranty claims, Technical Service Bulletins, NHTSA complaints, engineering notes, and component risk registries may each contain part of the truth, but no single intelligence layer synthesizes them into a fast, evidence-backed recall readiness view.

This system is designed for evidence synthesis, not answer generation. Its purpose is to compress the distance between fragmented signals and defensible recall decision support.

The project demonstrates a three-layer Agentic RAG architecture that retrieves from a static technical corpus, live regulatory-style complaint signals, and structured claim data. The system then generates a structured risk report with recall readiness level, confidence score, trend direction, key evidence, and a recommendation for engineering review.

System Scope

This design focuses on signal detection, evidence synthesis, and decision support. Final recall authority remains with engineering, legal, safety, and executive governance bodies. The Co-Pilot strengthens human decision-making; it does not replace regulated recall judgment.

Business Problem

The Hidden Cost of Reactive Recall Management

The capstone scenario frames Ford’s recall challenge around fragmented signal detection, slow escalation, and costly reactive decision-making. Warranty engineering and quality teams must interpret claim patterns, TSB activity, customer complaints, and regulatory triggers from separate systems before forming a recall readiness position.

$3.4B Annual warranty cost in the project scenario.
18–24 Months from field signal to recall decision in the reactive model.
50K+ NHTSA complaints received annually in the project framing.
$100M+ Estimated exposure per major reactive recall event.

Strategic Interpretation

This is not a document search problem. It is a decision-intelligence problem. The value comes from connecting scattered evidence into a structured recall readiness assessment before regulatory escalation, brand damage, or large-scale remediation costs increase.

02
Why Agentic RAG

Why Traditional ML and Basic Chatbots Are Not Enough

Traditional ML Limitation

Static, Retrospective, and Hard to Defend

  • Traditional ML models rely on fixed historical training data and can become stale as new complaints, claims, and TSBs emerge.
  • Safety decisions require current evidence, not only pattern recognition from old data.
  • A risk score without source traceability is difficult to defend across engineering, legal, and compliance review.
Agentic RAG Advantage

Current, Grounded, and Auditable

  • Agentic RAG retrieves current context at query time instead of relying only on model memory.
  • It decomposes a query, searches multiple evidence layers, ranks retrieved material, and synthesizes only from grounded context.
  • The result is not just an answer; it is a traceable decision-support artifact.

Design Principle

Recall intelligence requires freshness, explainability, and source grounding. RAG is not chosen because it is fashionable. It is chosen because the decision environment changes continuously and every recommendation must be auditable.

03
Architecture

An Azure-Native Agentic RAG Pipeline

The architecture follows a staged enterprise pipeline: source ingestion, chunking, embedding, vector indexing, multi-source retrieval, grounded synthesis, and structured risk output. The design is intentionally Azure-native so it can align with enterprise identity, observability, security, and deployment patterns.

Azure OpenAI GPT-4o synthesis and embedding generation for grounded recall intelligence.
Azure AI Search Vector and semantic retrieval over warranty, TSB, recall, and risk documents.
Agentic Retriever Query decomposition, multi-source search, relevance ranking, and context aggregation.
Structured Claim Store SQL-style retrieval over claim rates, model years, component categories, and trend direction.
NHTSA Signal Layer Live regulatory-style complaint and recall signal retrieval for freshness.
Human Review Layer Engineer validation, override capture, and feedback into retrieval scoring.

Failure Handling

If retrieval confidence is low, evidence is conflicting, or the supporting context is insufficient, the system defaults to “insufficient signal” and routes the case to human review instead of generating a forced conclusion.

04
Agent Reasoning

How the Agent Moves from Query to Evidence

Step 1

Query Decomposition

  • The agent breaks a user question into sub-tasks such as component lookup, claim trend search, complaint retrieval, historical recall comparison, and TSB correlation.
  • This prevents the system from treating a recall question as a simple keyword search.
Step 2

Parallel Multi-Source Retrieval

  • Static corpus passages are retrieved through vector search.
  • Structured claim records are queried for rates, trends, and model-year cohorts.
  • Regulatory-style complaint signals are retrieved for external signal confirmation.
Step 3

Context Aggregation and Ranking

  • Retrieved passages are merged into a unified evidence set.
  • Relevance, recency, severity, and source consistency determine which evidence is passed into synthesis.
Step 4

Grounded Synthesis

  • GPT-4o synthesizes a structured report using only retrieved evidence.
  • Unsupported claims are rejected by the system design.
  • The output becomes a reviewable decision-support artifact.
05
Decision Output

A Recall Intelligence Report, Not a Chatbot Answer

Every response is structured to support decision review. The Co-Pilot returns recall readiness risk, confidence score, trend direction, key signals, evidence references, and a specific recommendation. This format is intentionally designed for engineering committees, quality teams, legal review, and executive escalation.

Auditability

Outputs are structured to support auditability, including traceable evidence references, signal weighting rationale, and reproducible reasoning paths. A recommendation is only useful if reviewers can inspect why it was generated.

Decision Readiness

The system does not simply summarize documents. It converts scattered operational signals into a repeatable intelligence format that can support recall readiness discussions, investigation prioritization, and proactive escalation decisions.

Example Logic

A high-risk output may combine rising NHTSA complaints, a matching historical recall pattern, increasing warranty claims, and an active TSB that has not reduced incident rates. The recommendation is grounded in converging signals rather than a single isolated metric.

06
Grounding Corpus

A Synthetic but Enterprise-Realistic Ford Evidence Layer

The project corpus is built from synthetic but structurally realistic Ford documents designed to simulate how enterprise knowledge would support recall readiness reasoning. The corpus includes warranty policy, TSBs, historical warranty claims, recall decision rules, and a component risk registry.

Policy & Decision Layer

Warranty and Recall Governance

  • Warranty policy defines coverage boundaries, eligibility rules, exclusions, and claim submission requirements.
  • Recall decision rules define claim-rate thresholds, complaint spike triggers, similarity to prior recall patterns, and legal escalation conditions.
Technical Evidence Layer

TSBs, Claim Trends, and Risk Registry

  • TSBs provide symptoms, root causes, repair procedures, claim trends, and NHTSA references.
  • Claim summaries provide vehicle-family trends, component rates, and historical recall correlations.
  • Risk registry records component watch status, composite risk score, trend direction, and threshold distance to recall trigger.

Why the Corpus Matters

A RAG system is only as strong as the knowledge structure behind it. This corpus gives the Co-Pilot a realistic grounding layer for policy interpretation, technical symptom matching, component risk comparison, and recall readiness classification.

07
Validation & Responsible AI

Trust Is Built Into the Architecture

Grounded Generation

Unsupported Claims Are Blocked

  • The system enforces grounded generation by restricting outputs to retrieved context.
  • If the retrieved evidence is insufficient, the system returns a manual review state instead of inventing a conclusion.
  • This mitigates hallucination at the architecture level, not only at the prompt level.
Human Oversight

Engineer-in-the-Loop Review

  • High-risk recommendations require warranty engineer validation.
  • Overrides and validation outcomes can feed back into relevance scoring.
  • Periodic audits compare recommendations against actual recall outcomes and committee decisions.

Responsible AI Positioning

The Co-Pilot is not designed to replace recall committees, legal counsel, or safety engineers. It is designed to compress evidence gathering, improve signal visibility, and make human review faster, more traceable, and more consistent.
