Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning in the Automotive Industry

A real-world comparative case study showing why traditional machine learning fits structured business prediction problems while deep learning becomes essential for raw, sensor-driven autonomy.

This artifact compares GM OnStar subscriber churn prediction with Waymo autonomous driving to demonstrate how the right AI approach depends on data type, interpretability, and operational context.

Project Overview

This project analyzes how different machine learning approaches apply to real-world automotive scenarios. It contrasts structured prediction problems with unstructured, real-time decision-making environments.

What This Shows

Demonstrates how choosing the right AI model depends on data structure, interpretability, scalability, and business objectives — not just model complexity.

Tools & Concepts

Traditional ML (SVM, Gradient Boosting), Deep Learning (CNNs, Reinforcement Learning), structured vs unstructured data, and enterprise AI decision frameworks.

Why This Matters

Shows your ability to think like a Solution Architect — aligning AI models with business use cases rather than blindly applying deep learning everywhere.
