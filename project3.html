Project 03

Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning in the Automotive Industry

A real-world comparative case study showing why traditional machine learning fits structured business prediction problems while deep learning becomes essential for raw, sensor-driven autonomy.

This artifact compares GM OnStar subscriber churn prediction with Waymo autonomous driving to show that the right AI approach depends on data type, interpretability needs, and operational context.

00
Context & Foundation

Introduction

Machine learning and deep learning are often discussed as if one has replaced the other. In practice, they solve different classes of problems. This project uses the automotive industry to make that distinction concrete, comparing a structured customer prediction task with a safety-critical autonomous driving task.

The right model is not the most advanced one — it is the one that best matches the problem, the data, the business need, and the consequences of failure.

By pairing GM OnStar churn prediction with Waymo autonomous driving, this artifact moves beyond theory and shows why one approach excels where the other would struggle. Traditional ML performs strongly when inputs are tabular and explainability matters, while deep learning becomes necessary when perception depends on raw, high-dimensional data.

01
Comparative Framework

Why One Works Where the Other Does Not

Traditional Machine Learning

GM OnStar Churn Prediction

  • Uses structured customer data such as subscription history, usage frequency, billing patterns, and service interactions.
  • Supports explainable risk scoring that business teams can act on with confidence.
  • Fits tabular data where domain experts can identify meaningful features in advance.
  • Works efficiently with lower computational demand and moderate data volume.
Deep Learning

Waymo Autonomous Driving

  • Processes unstructured raw inputs from cameras, LiDAR, and radar.
  • Learns complex features automatically without depending on manual engineering.
  • Supports perception and navigation in dynamic environments where rules are not enough.
  • Requires extremely large data volume, high compute, and sophisticated training pipelines.

Key Comparison

Traditional machine learning is a business-friendly choice when the problem is structured and interpretability matters. Deep learning is the only practical path when the environment is perceptual, unstructured, and too complex for manually engineered features.

02
Case Study One

Why Traditional ML Fits GM OnStar

Business Problem

GM’s OnStar service depends on retaining subscribers across connected vehicle offerings. Predicting churn early allows the business to intervene before customers cancel, protecting recurring revenue and supporting proactive retention strategy.

Why the Method Fits

The inputs are structured and well understood: contract length, usage logs, billing patterns, vehicle age, and support history. These are exactly the kinds of fields traditional ML models like SVM and gradient boosting are built to handle effectively.

Interpretability Advantage

Marketing and operations teams need to understand why a subscriber is considered at risk. Traditional ML provides a level of transparency that supports targeted action, such as incentives, reminders, or personalized retention outreach.

Why Deep Learning Is Not the Right Fit

For a structured churn prediction task, deep learning adds complexity without meaningful value. It requires more data and compute, while reducing explainability in a use case where business trust and practical actionability matter.

03
Case Study Two

Why Deep Learning Fits Waymo

Autonomous Driving Context

Waymo operates in an environment where the system must interpret roads, pedestrians, signals, vehicles, and hazards from live sensor input. The problem is not merely predictive — it is perceptual, dynamic, and safety dependent.

Why the Method Fits

Deep learning can process high-dimensional raw data from cameras, LiDAR, and radar, learning visual and spatial features automatically. CNNs support perception, while reinforcement learning contributes to navigation and decision behavior in complex driving environments.

Scale & Complexity

Waymo’s system benefits from millions of real-world miles and billions of simulation miles. That scale is critical because autonomous driving requires exposure to rare edge cases, unpredictable environments, and constantly shifting conditions.

Why Traditional ML Breaks Down

Traditional ML depends on manually engineered features. In raw image and sensor streams, that quickly becomes impractical. Too much information is lost in preprocessing, and the system cannot match the richness required for safe real-time perception.

04
Final Synthesis

The Strategic Takeaway

This comparison matters because AI selection is not about choosing the trendiest model. It is about selecting the method that fits the structure of the data, the need for explanation, the scale of the environment, and the operational stakes involved.
