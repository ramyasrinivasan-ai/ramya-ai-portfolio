Project 03

Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning in the Automotive Industry

A real-world comparative case study showing why traditional machine learning fits structured business prediction problems while deep learning becomes essential for raw, sensor-driven autonomy.

This artifact compares GM OnStar subscriber churn prediction with Waymo autonomous driving to show that the right AI approach depends on data type, interpretability needs, computational demands, and operational context.

Traditional ML Deep Learning Automotive Industry Case Study Analysis
00
Context & Foundation

Introduction

Machine learning and deep learning are often grouped together as if one naturally replaces the other. In practice, they serve different purposes. This artifact uses the automotive industry to make that distinction concrete by comparing a structured business prediction problem with a raw-sensor autonomous driving problem.

The strongest AI decision is not choosing the most advanced model. It is choosing the model that best fits the data, the business objective, the need for explainability, and the consequence of failure.

By pairing GM OnStar churn prediction with Waymo autonomous driving, this project moves beyond abstract theory. It demonstrates that traditional machine learning and deep learning are each powerful in the right context, but ineffective when applied to the wrong one.

01
Comparative Framework

Why One Works Where the Other Does Not

Traditional Machine Learning

GM OnStar Churn Prediction

  • Works with structured customer records such as usage frequency, billing patterns, and subscription history.
  • Supports explainability, which helps business teams understand why a customer is marked at risk.
  • Requires moderate data volume and lower compute cost.
  • Fits retention and classification problems where features can be clearly defined.
Deep Learning

Waymo Autonomous Driving

  • Processes raw, unstructured data from cameras, LiDAR, and radar.
  • Learns useful features automatically instead of relying on manual engineering.
  • Needs massive data volume and high computational power.
  • Fits perception-heavy tasks where real-time interpretation of complex environments is essential.

Strategic Distinction

Traditional ML is strongest when the data is structured and decisions must be explainable. Deep learning becomes necessary when the environment is perceptual, dynamic, and too information-rich for human-engineered features.

02
Case Study One

Why Traditional ML Fits GM OnStar

Business Problem

GM’s OnStar connected vehicle service depends on recurring subscribers. Predicting churn before cancellation allows GM to take proactive retention action and protect revenue.

Why the Method Fits

The available inputs are structured and tabular: contract terms, usage frequency, service history, billing activity, and vehicle information. These are exactly the conditions where traditional ML methods such as SVM and gradient boosting perform effectively.

Interpretability Advantage

Business teams need more than a prediction. They need a reason they can act on. Traditional ML supports that need by making churn drivers easier to explain and connect to retention strategy.

Why Deep Learning Is Not Ideal Here

Deep learning would introduce more complexity, higher computational cost, and reduced explainability without meaningful benefit for a structured churn prediction task.

03
Case Study Two

Why Deep Learning Fits Waymo

Autonomous Driving Context

Waymo operates in complex environments where the system must interpret roads, people, signals, vehicles, and hazards from live sensor streams with no human intervention.

Why the Method Fits

Deep learning can extract meaningful patterns from raw visual and spatial data, enabling object recognition, scene understanding, and real-time navigation decisions in ways traditional ML cannot scale to match.

Scale & Complexity

Autonomous driving requires training on millions of real-world miles and billions of simulated miles, because edge cases and rare conditions are critical to safety. Deep learning improves as that scale grows.

Why Traditional ML Breaks Down

Traditional ML depends on manually engineered features. In raw sensor environments, that approach cannot capture the complexity of real-world driving without major information loss and reduced reliability.

Final Synthesis

The Real Takeaway

This artifact shows that AI selection should be driven by problem structure, data characteristics, explainability requirements, and operational stakes. In practice, the best solution is rarely the most fashionable one — it is the one that fits.
